Export Your Subtitle Layback With Compressor

Exporting the subtitled video

Once your subtitle layback is ready to export in Final Cut Pro X, you can use Compressor,
which is the compression tool used by Final Cut Pro X to export videos.
Compressor allows you to do advanced encoding very easily.
You can find tutorials for it on the web and there’s also a lot of documentation and help within the software itself.

1.-To export a Project in Final Cut Pro X just select the Project. Then, from the Menu Bar on the top of the screen,
select “File” > “Send to Compressor.”

Compressor is launched with your subtitled Project loaded.

2.-On the left side you will see two sets of encoders: BUILT-IN and CUSTOM encoders. Expand BUILT-IN encoders
to see a huge collection of encoders for most cases. Select the one that best fits your task and drag it
to the Batch Area over your recently loaded Project.

If you are and advanced user, you can do your own encoders and fine-tune them.

3.-Select your project from its own box on the Batch Area.
You can customize it and/or choose the location where you want to save your encoded file by right-clicking on it.

4.-Below the Batch Area you will find the “Start Batch” button. Press it to start the encoding process.
