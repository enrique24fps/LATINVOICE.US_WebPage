Setting Up Your Subtitle Layback in Final Cut Pro X

Step by Step Guide

Final Cut Pro X (FCPX) is maybe the best and more affordable solution to work with video at a professional level.
You won’t get better results by using any other solution out there, you have all you need right there.

There are many tutorials around, you can check them on the web and many of them are for free.
FCPX, of course, has a very extensive documentation and you can always find support on the web.

This is a very specific and short guide to import an XML file. You should at least take a look at the “Interface Overview”
in the FCP X Help to properly follow this guide.

1-Open FCPX.

2-Make sure to open a Library to work with and select it.
If you haven’t opened a Library yet, select “File” from the Menu bar (top of the screen) and then “Open Library”.
If this option is not available, create a new Library. On the Menu bar, select “File” > “New” > “Library.”

3-Once you have a selected Library to work with, go to the menu bar and select “File” > “Import” > “XML.”
A file browser will pop up. Select your XML file, which should have a .fcpxml extension, and click on “Import.”

4.-An Event and a Project with the names you gave them on step 1 will be imported into your Library.
Imports can take long, so be patient. For example, if you import one thousand subtitles, it can take up to 5 minutes to import.
It’s a lot of information to process, but you will be saving yourself a lot of work.

5-Make sure your recently imported Event is selected so you can see the project with the subtitle layback.
Double-click on the Project’s name to open its timeline. Preparation of the display will take a few seconds,
after which you will be able to see a timeline with your subtitles ready to run.

6.-If you haven’t imported your video yet, drag it from Finder to your Event window or to your Event name on the Libraries window.
You can also select “File” > “Import” > “Media” from the Menu Bar or press cmd + i.
7.-On the bottom part of your Project’s timeline, below the subtitles, you will see a black bar,
which will be replaced by your video. Drag your video over this bar until it is highlighted.
Drop it and you will see a context menu. Select “Replace".

You can also drop your video under this black bar, but make sure it starts right at the beginning of the timeline.
Now you can select and delete the black bar.

8.-Your subtitle layback is ready. You can now play it by selecting the playhead on the timeline and hit the space bar.
Now make sure that you used the right timecode by spotting your video and checking that your subtitles are still synced.
If they’re not, assuming that the source timing is right, you may want to make an adjustment on step 3 > option 1
by applying a correction factor.

9.-Once your QC is done, you are ready to export your video with Compressor.

Recommended: watch the tutorial video on SUBTITLE TO FCPXML’s web page. A link is provided at the bottom of this page.
